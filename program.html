EROS, Jan. 21, 2018

Introduction and Overview

The Chairperson of ACM discussed the reason for the monthly meeting, as well as the benefits of having them. The Chair of the Computer Science department also gave a presentation in regards to his support and enthusiasm for ACM and what we stand for. The meetings are designed for students to connect with peers, as well as professionals in the industry. Every meeting there will be a presenter who comes in and shares their expertise in a certain field and possibly offer students internships, if there are any available at the company they work for.

Freelance and Browser Hacking

Chad Clites, a former USD Computer Science graduate, discussed freelance work and the different paths one may take with a Computer Science degree. Clites is currently a Web Developer who founded Extant.Digital and has been doing freelance work for many different companies; he also has been designing and developing websites for individuals. He gave insight to students about how to manage one's own business, as well as how to interact with customers. Clites also introduced various tools with regards to editing JavaScript and CSS within a browser, as well as changing the design of the webpage with small hacks he often employs.

BioInformatics

Dr. Erliang Zeng, a Biology Professor at USD, gave a presentation about the role of computer science in Biology. Dr. Zeng got his PhD in computer science and his MS and BS in Biology. He incorporates his 2 interests into one--Bioinformatics. He currently directs the BioComs Lab and talked about some of the research he has been focusing on and is looking for students possibly interested in assistantships in bioinformatics.
